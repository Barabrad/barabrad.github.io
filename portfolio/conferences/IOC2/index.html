IOC II

Conference Topic

The 2nd International Orbital Debris Conference is focused on orbital debris, and it had speakers from across the world.

Presentation Topic

While doing research under Dr. Michael Kezirian, we started looking into the implications of orbital debris in lunar and cislunar orbit after my sophomore year. By finding the cost in fuel for making collision avoidance maneuvers, we could provide guidance for setting requirements on the tracking of orbital debris in lunar and cislunar space.

Process

Using the Artemis I mission as a baseline trajectory, I made a model of a lunar mission in STK (Systems Tool Kit). Following my professor's guidance, I ran multiple maneuvers at a point in cislunar space and right before the spacecraft enters lunar orbit, finding the relationships between the time (between the burn and the "collision"), separation distance, and maneuver magnitude (measured in velocity change). To find some of the trend curves, I made a curve-fitter that could work with multiple parameters.

From there, we began developing the paper and slide deck for the conference.

Results

On December 4, 2023, I presented our work at the conference.

After the conference, we began fine-tuning the paper for publication in the Journal of Space Safety Engineering. Many iterations later, the paper was published.
